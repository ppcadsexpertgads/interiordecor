Why In-Home Bars Are Making a Comeback

Kimberly Coffman · Dec 1, 2023 · Leave a Comment

Step into the modern age of stylish entertaining and hospitality with in-home bars. Today, homeowners are embracing the revival of this classic trend to express their unique style and elevate their hosting prowess.

In the mid-20th century, in-home bars epitomized chic, sociable living, providing the perfect setting for cocktail parties and casual get-togethers. These days we’re witnessing a resurgence of the at-home bar.

The reasons for this revival are varied, such as the rising popularity of craft cocktails and microbreweries, which encourage aficionados to create their own inventive concoctions. Additionally, the lingering effects of the global pandemic have inspired the transformation of the home into multifunctional spaces, pushing us to seek ways to incorporate more entertainment elements.

This article will explore the growing trend of in-home bars and provide bar home ideas. By the end, you’ll understand why they are reclaiming their place in modern homes and, perhaps, feel inspired to add one to your home.

What is a home bar?

A home bar is a dedicated space within a home designed to store and serve beverages. It often consists of storage for a variety of drinks, glassware, bar tools, a surface for mixing drinks, and sometimes seating options. Creating a bar in your home provides a canvas to showcase your unique style and uplevel your hosting game.


In terms of design and functionality, in-home bars can be categorized into two main types: wet bars and dry bars.

A wet bar’s setup has a sink with running water, a larger counter for preparing drinks, and often features built-in under-counter appliances like refrigerators or wine coolers.

A dry bar offers a convenient space without the need for a water supply and drain system. It offers storage for bottles, glassware, tools, and a surface to prepare drinks. Dry bars do not require a standalone piece of furniture and can easily be integrated as a seamless part of any room.

Convenience is one of the major benefits of in-home bars. With a small home bar, your
favorite cocktail or brew is only a few steps away. It’s a wonderful spot for entertaining
guests. Moreover, a home bar setting can be a creative way to express personal style,
showcasing your taste.

Types of in-home bars

Just as homes come in various styles, so do in-home bars. The options include built-in bars, wooden bars, small bars, home mini bars, and freestanding bars.

A built-in bar is designed as part of the home’s architectural plan. It features cabinetry, countertops, and appliances to mimic a professional bar setup. A wooden bar offers a traditional and rustic feel, often crafted from solid wood with elaborate detailing.

A mini bar is perfect if your room is small or you prefer a minimalist design. It can be a stylish cabinet storing a curated collection of beverages. If you prefer flexibility, a freestanding bar is a great choice.

The style of an in-home bar depends on your lifestyle, personal taste, available space, and budget. It’s a fantastic way to carve out a space to relax both alone and with guests, as it can be customized to any size and decorated with tiles, wall hangings, and decorative pieces to create a unique vibe that aligns with your personal style.

A home bar should be easily accessible yet not obstruct regular movement. A bar cart is ideal if you have a very tight budget or limited room. It offers versatility and mobility and can add a vintage charm to your living room. You can adorn your bar cart with your favorite spirits, glassware, an ice bucket, and other bar essentials.

When it comes to your budget, the amount you can invest will determine the size, features, and materials used in your bar’s construction. For instance, designing a built-in bar is a more comprehensive project involving plumbing (in the case of a wet bar), electrical wiring, and custom carpentry, thus requiring a larger budget. On the other hand, a dry bar setup such as a freestanding or mini bar requires less infrastructure, resulting in a more affordable alternative.

Similarly, the choice of materials will affect the overall cost. High-end finishes like marble countertops, bespoke woodwork, or premium appliances will significantly increase your budget compared to more modest alternatives.

Living room bars

Bars for the living room are an excellent choice if you love to entertain in a casual yet stylish setting. This integration transforms your living room into a dynamic entertainment hub, making it easy for guests to mingle and enjoy a drink.

Some popular features of living room bars include minimalist cabinetry, floating shelves to showcase premium bottles, and integrated LED lighting. In terms of color schemes, darker tones like deep blues, grays, or black add a layer of sophistication.

One key benefit of a living room bar is the convenience and sociability it offers. Your guests are no longer confined to a kitchen island or dining table but can freely engage with each other in a relaxed setting.

Alternatively, you can opt for a mini bar in your living room for a more dedicated and compact option. Unlike a bar cart, a mini bar is a self-contained piece of furniture with specific storage for a limited selection of beverages and bar accessories. It provides a fixed and organized station for your drinks, ensuring everything is conveniently within reach.

Dining room bar ideas

Some dining room bar trends include elegant designs to complement the formal dining atmosphere. Built-in credenzas, mirrored backsplashes, and glass-fronted cabinets are popular, adding a touch of glamor while showcasing your collection.

The primary advantage of a dining room bar is the smooth transition it provides between meals and after-dinner drinks. However, one potential downside could be the bar dominating the dining area, especially in smaller rooms, which could create a bottleneck in the flow of traffic to and from the kitchen or infringe on guests’ personal space.

The room’s architectural layout must be considered when designing a dining room bar. For example, if the dining room is close to the kitchen, the bar might ideally be placed in the transition space, serving both areas efficiently.

A compact corner bar or a sleek sideboard turned into a drinks station can be an excellent solution for smaller rooms. A well-equipped bar cabinet or a freestanding bar can make a striking statement for larger rooms.

You can also turn your dining room into a bar area, where a section or the entire room becomes dedicated to the bar. This is particularly appealing if you frequently host social events.

Kitchen Bars

Kitchen bars are a great solution for optimizing space while adding functionality and style. Kitchen bar designs range from built-in under-counter wine fridges to bespoke cocktail cabinets.

One key advantage of a kitchen bar is that it promotes a more social atmosphere in the home, allowing people to enjoy each other’s company while cooking and dining. However, it’s important to consider possible limitations, like the size of the counter and potential clutter or spills that may cross over into your cooking area.

When creating a kitchen bar, prioritize organization and ease of access. A well-designed kitchen bar can significantly relieve congestion in front of the refrigerator by providing a dedicated space for your beverages. This smart setup effectively moves people away from the main refrigerator, especially during parties when guests tend to gravitate to the kitchen. Guests will appreciate having ease of access to a mix of additional non-alcoholic drinks inside a mini fridge.

Including a mini fridge within the kitchen bar provides convenient access to all drinks, reducing the need to access the main refrigerator frequently. Additionally, you can incorporate a coffee bar station into your kitchen bar, further dispersing congestion from the cooking area.

Other things you can install include a wine rack, drawer dividers for glassware, or even a small sink for washing barware. If you don’t have much room, a corner of your countertop can be transformed into a dedicated bar area, or you can opt for a kitchen wall bar – typically involving utilizing vertical space by installing open shelving, wall-mounted racks, or cabinets.

Bedroom Bars

For some, having a bar in the bedroom might seem unconventional. Still, it’s an emerging trend that offers the luxury of enjoying a relaxing nightcap or fresh cup of coffee with your morning routine without having to leave the comfort of your bedroom. Bedroom bars feature compact setups with streamlined storage, open shelving, mini-fridges, and coffee machines.

Despite the benefits of convenience, a bedroom wet bar may have potential challenges like space constraints or the risk of spillages in the bedroom. However, there are several ways to accommodate this risk, such as using a bar mat, installing an arm rail, or placing a washable rug in front of the bar.
If you’re considering a bedroom bar, it’s essential to carefully plan the layout to ensure it blends seamlessly into your room without becoming obtrusive. Opt for sleek appliances that run quietly to avoid disrupting your peaceful retreat.

Conclusion

The concept of in-home bars is making a remarkable comeback. The allure of crafting bespoke cocktails, the desire for convenience and entertainment within the comfort of our own homes, and the ever-changing landscape of our living areas have all contributed to the rising popularity of in-home bars.

Whether it’s a built-in bar that exhibits sleek modernity, a wooden bar radiating rustic charm, a minimalist mini bar, or a flexible freestanding bar, there’s a design to cater to every taste, lifestyle, and space. Let’s not forget the versatile bar cart, an affordable and trendy option that fits virtually any room.

If you’re looking to infuse a touch of luxury, style, and convenience into your home, consider joining the trend and adding an in-home bar. Remember, the best bar designs reflect your style and preferences and serve a functional purpose tailored to your needs. In-home bars are far more than just storage for beverages. They’re a testament to evolving lifestyles, a tribute to unique tastes, and a celebration of the joy of entertaining.
